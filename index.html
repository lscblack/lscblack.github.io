We Are The Evolvers

ALU E-LAB Evolvers: Unlocking Minds, Shaping Tomorrow. With education as our key and teamwork as our driving force, we embrace the ever-changing world. Together, we empower minds to strive for excellence, breaking barriers to create a stronger future. As a community dedicated to growth, we harness the power of innovation and collaboration, paving the way for transformative learning experiences. With open minds and united hearts, we embark on a journey of endless possibilities, embracing challenges and embracing change. Join us in our mission to evolve, as we forge a path to a brighter, more connected, and thriving world."

CHALLENGES OF E-LAB

In our time in E-Lab we completed 7 challenges

1. ALUxpress

In this challenge we did a scavenger hunt trying to find different locations around campus

2. Introduction

In this challenge we had to give an elevator pitch on what we're passionate about, why, and what we want to do to solve it.

3. Discover Africa

In this challenge we had to write a story about an African character through the lens of a community. We chose to write Prince Louis Rwagasore's story.

4. Help-Lab

In this challenge we had to find a cause we cared about in our community and done something to help our community. We decided to clean the roads in our community.

5. Hunt for Treasure

In this challenge we went to interview MTN Agents about the factors that influenced their earnings.

6. Launch Your Mission V2

In this challenge we were told to assume we were given $1M to implement our solution to our chosen problem of low quality of education and explain the expenditures involved.

7. Digital Print

The 7th and final challenge is the one you are seeing right now with this website we created to showcase our E-Lab journey.

ABOUT OUR MISSION

We decided to tackle the grand challenge of education in Rwanda.

Mission Statement

Our think tank mission is to improve the standard of education in Rwanda through advocacy and training of teachers in order to have well-trained teachers that can identify and address the unique needs of their students.

Problem Statement

In Rwandan public primary schools teachers are often poorly trained and sometimes even not trained at all, with UNICEF reporting that about 20% of primary and secondary teachers don’t even have the basic requirements. This can lead to students feeling disengaged from the learning process and having lower grades and self-esteem.
